Why Washington State Stands Out?

  • Mountainous Terrain

    • Bigfoot's habitat is believed to be in the mountains, because the terrain can provide useful camouflage to help hide from humans that might try to seach for it. Washington State happens to have such an environment for Bigfoot, having the 4th highest average elevation in the whole country. The highest elevation in the Washington State is 14,411 ft

  • Suitable Prey

    • Bigfoot is believed to prey on some mammals such as deer and elk. This is also what Washington State is well known for, being the home of at least 270,000 deer and 60,000 elk.

  • Law Protection

    • In the Skamania County, the law states that the county is a Sasquatch refuge. People who try to hunt it may be punished with a misdemeanor or a gross misdemeanor and may be sentenced to a year in the county jail or a fine of up to $1,000.
